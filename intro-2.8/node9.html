The Linear Programming Process

The linear programming (LP) process is the most complex and computationally intensive of the five processes. Its job is to perform the bounding and branching operations. These operations are, of course, central to the performance of the algorithm.

Functions Performed by the Linear Programming Process

  • Inform the tree manager when a new subproblem is needed.
  • Receive a subproblem. Process the subproblem in conjunction with the cut generator and the cut pool.
  • If necessary, choose a branching object and send its description back to the tree manager.
